## 📚 Topics Covered by Notes

### 1.) Basic Electrodynamics
Covering the basics of electrostatics and magnetostatics from Griffiths to get a clear base for plasma physics. (Dynamics were covered too just notes are unavailable).

### 2.) Elementary Plasma Physics
The essence of Nuclear Fusion, cross-sections, mean-free path, reactor power balance, etc. Single particle behaviour in fields so Larmor orbits, Polarization drifts and more. Collisional analysis of particle and classical scattering. 

### 3.) Non-Fluid MHD Models
Just covering the basics of the two-fluid model and how we can generalize the microscopic collisional analysis picture into a macroscopic fluid. This is where you learn how the plasma acts like a fancy fluid (oversimplified).

### 4.) MHD
This is my favourite part about plasma physics. Reduce the two-fluid model using some assumptions into a single fluid plasma model. Investigate force balance and equilibrium conditions with MHD relevant to confinement fusion. Learnt about Z, theta, screw pinches and how its hard to confine a plasma magnetically.
